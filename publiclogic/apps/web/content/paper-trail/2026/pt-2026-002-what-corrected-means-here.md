## The rule

A published item's body is never silently edited. Any substantive change — and, by policy, any change at all, down to a typo — gets three things: a new entry appended to the corrections log, a version bump, and an updated `dateModified`. The log is never collapsed and never trimmed. If you read this item a year from now, every correction we've ever made to it is still sitting below, in order, dated.

## This item is the proof

This is a Finding, not a Release, and the shelf label above says so correctly — but it didn't say so in the first version we published. We wrote "Release" in the second paragraph by mistake, caught it, and corrected it the same day. Rather than quietly fixing the word and moving on, we logged it: see the corrections log on this page for the exact entry, dated and versioned.

That's a small mistake on purpose. The point isn't the typo — it's that you can verify, on the record, that PublicLogic's corrections doctrine does what we say it does the first time it was ever tested in public.

## What this doesn't cover

Corrections aren't retractions. If an item needs to be withdrawn rather than fixed, it stays at its URL with a retraction banner and its own correction entry — it does not 404. We haven't needed that yet. When we do, it will look exactly as unglamorous as this one does.
