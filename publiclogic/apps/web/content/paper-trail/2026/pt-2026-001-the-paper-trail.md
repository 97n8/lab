## Why a paper trail

PublicLogic exists to make institutions more capable of carrying their own work forward. That only holds if our own record does the same thing we ask of our clients': stays dated, stays versioned, and stays honest about what changed and when.

The Paper Trail is where that record lives. It carries two shelves:

- **Releases** — announcements and version-of-record statements: the things we said, dated and citable.
- **Findings** — sourced analysis and documented observations, cited and correctable.

## What makes an item a record, not a post

Every item here ships with an audit line — its record ID, version, and publish date — visible at the top of the page, not buried in a byline. Every abstract is written to be copied into an email as-is. Every citation is a single string you can paste into a bibliography. Every item ships a sealed PDF, so the version you read today is the version you can hold a year from now.

None of that is decoration. It's the same discipline PuddleJumper (our Governance Process Runtime) applies to a case record: a thing is only a record if you can point to exactly what it said, exactly when, and exactly what changed since.

## Corrections are the point, not the exception

If something we publish here turns out to be wrong, incomplete, or just needs a clearer sentence, we don't quietly edit it. We append a correction, bump the version, and leave the log visible on the page — forever, never collapsed. A retraction doesn't make an item disappear either; it stays at its URL with a banner and a correction entry. Removed-content 404s are the one thing we've ruled out from the start.

This is the founding record of that discipline. Everything published under The Paper Trail from here follows the same rule this item does.
